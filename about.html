About

I don't have as much programming experience as some other developers out there, but I am eager to learn and willing to work hard to improve. My education has been a combination of teaching myself and going to school. I have learned more from personal projects than I have ever learned from school, not to mention how much more enjoyable it is to work on something that I am passionate about. I am a self-motivated individual and think that I can offer a lot to any company willing to hire me. What I lack in experience I make up for with my work ethic and a willingness to learn. Looking through my projects you might notice that there are a fair amount of video games. This is mostly because I find making video games to be a very enjoyable and rewarding experience. Since I was young, I have enjoyed video games. This eventually led to a passion for making them. For the past couple of years, I have taught myself how to make video games, mostly using the Unity game engine, though I have also used OpenGL (GLUT). One of the first projects that I ever finished was a game I made in a weekend that I eventually put on the Google Play store. The game, "Cal-Transmission," has a page of this website dedicated to it. The game is very simple, but the experience taught me a lot and only motivated me to make more games. I am currently working on a 2D platformer that is much more involved than Cal-Transmission. The game doesn't have a name yet, but the working title is "Raccoon Game." The game is my attempt at telling a story, though it is currently in the early stages of development, I think it shows my passion for software development. If you are interested, there are more details about the games on their pages.

While video games are my primary passion when it comes to software development, I find many other subjects interesting and I am more than willing to learn something less familiar. I have taught myself some web development in an attempt to satisfy my curiosity(HTML, CSS, JS). Through school, I have become familiar with c++ and somewhat c and python. Along with videogames being fun to make, they have also taught me a lot about how to utilize OOP practices. Through my formal education, I have also been introduced to algorithms, data structures, calculus, linear algebra, and statistics.

All in, I think that I would be a valuable addition to any development team. I am relatively new to the industry, but everyone has to start somewhere and I am hard-working and willing to learn. If you think I would be a good fit with your team, my email is listed at the bottom of every page and I check it regularly.
